What We Heard | Strategy & Customer Experience Context

Strategy Theme

Digital-First Islamic Banking

DIB aims to become the leading digital Islamic bank in the UAE, competing with neobanks while maintaining full Shariah compliance across every product and journey.

Customer Experience

Fragmented Channel Journeys

Customers starting Murabaha financing online must restart in-branch. The digital and physical channels operate as separate worlds, creating frustration and drop-off.

The Bridge

Employees Are the Connection

Your employees are the bridge between strategy and customer experience. When they struggle with fragmented tools, customers feel it in longer wait times, repeated information, and inconsistent service.

Employee Experience Use Cases

Mapped to personas — from pain to solution

Frontline: Branch & Contact Center

Customer Service Officer (~450) • Contact Center Agent (~180) • Relationship Manager (~200)

Pain Points

  • 12-13 systems per customer interaction — constant screen switching steals 40% of interaction time
  • No visibility into customer's digital journey; customers must repeat themselves at every touchpoint
  • Average handle time of 8+ minutes for simple inquiries that should take 2 minutes
  • Shariah compliance as a separate manual step adds 2-5 minutes to every financing interaction

Challenges

  • Channel silos prevent seamless handoffs between mobile, branch, and contact center
  • 5+ week training for new hires due to 13-system landscape; 22% annual turnover amplifies cost
  • Cannot proactively identify cross-sell or advisory opportunities — service remains reactive
  • Bilingual (Arabic/English) operations require switching language settings per system
How might we give frontline staff a single, unified view of every customer interaction, product, and Shariah eligibility?
How might we enable seamless conversation continuity when customers move between mobile, branch, and contact center?
How might we embed Shariah compliance into guided workflows so it disappears as a separate friction step?
How might we reduce new-hire time-to-competency from 5+ weeks to under 2 weeks?
Use Case 1 • Frontline

Unified Advisor Desktop

Single screen combining customer profile, product holdings, interaction history, and Shariah-compliant product eligibility. Eliminates switching between T24, CRM, and compliance systems for every interaction.

Est. savings: $1.8M/yr
CX impact: Inquiry time drops from 6 min to 1.5 min; customer feels known and valued
Use Case 2 • Frontline

Omnichannel Customer Context

Agents and CSOs see the customer's complete digital journey: app sessions, online applications in progress, branch visits, and previous call notes. Conversations continue across channels without restarting.

Est. savings: $620K/yr
CX impact: Seamless channel transitions; 15-20% reduction in repeat contacts
Use Case 3 • Frontline

Shariah-Smart Guided Workflows

Step-by-step financing workflows with embedded Shariah compliance checks, auto-populated customer data from EID, and integrated e-signature. Reduces Murabaha processing from 40 min to 15 min. New employees follow the same guided process, cutting training time by 50%.

Est. savings: $740K/yr
CX impact: Financing decisions same-day instead of multi-day; new hires deliver consistent quality from week 1
Total Frontline Opportunity
$3.16M/yr

Back Office: Operations & Compliance

Operations Specialist • Shariah Compliance Officer • Processing Staff (~300 employees)

Pain Points

  • Manual Shariah audit trails require documentation across 3-4 separate systems for every review
  • Exception handling relies on email chains with no structured workflow or SLA tracking
  • Transaction disputes take 15+ minutes to file and have no end-to-end status visibility
  • CBUAE regulatory reporting requires manual data aggregation from multiple sources

Challenges

  • No structured case management — requests get lost between departments, SLAs breach silently
  • STP rate of only 42% means 58% of transactions require manual intervention
  • 6.5% processing error rate due to manual data re-entry across disconnected systems
  • KYC refresh and compliance documentation scattered across DMS, email, and audit logs
How might we create end-to-end case visibility from customer request through to resolution with automatic SLA tracking?
How might we automate Shariah audit trails so compliance documentation doesn't slow down processing?
How might we increase straight-through processing from 42% to 70%+ by eliminating manual re-keying?
How might we give operations staff real-time dashboards to prevent SLA breaches before they happen?
Use Case 4 • Back Office

Intelligent Case & Exception Management

End-to-end case tracking for disputes, KYC reviews, and transaction exceptions with SLA monitoring, automated routing, and built-in escalation paths. Specialists see full request history without email chains.

Est. savings: $480K/yr
CX impact: Customers receive proactive status updates; dispute resolution time halved
Use Case 5 • Back Office

Automated Compliance & STP Engine

Automated Shariah audit trail generation, KYC refresh workflows, and straight-through processing for standard transactions. Reduces manual re-keying, cuts error rate from 6.5% to under 2.5%, and frees compliance officers for exception-only reviews.

Est. savings: $360K/yr
CX impact: Faster processing of financing applications and account requests; fewer error-driven delays

Hypotheses for Validation

H1 | System Switching Tax

System switching accounts for 25-30% of DIB employee productive time

With 13 systems in the landscape and an average of 4.1 switches per task, DIB employees spend an estimated $5M+ annually on context switching. Validation: Track actual switching time for 10 common tasks across 3 branches.

Source: HBR/Qatalog research; UAE banking benchmarks
H2 | First Contact Resolution

Contact center FCR is below 65% due to lack of omnichannel context

Without visibility into digital banking activity, agents cannot resolve inquiries that span channels. Each unresolved contact generates 1.5-2.0 additional contacts at AED 25-35 each.

Source: Industry benchmark; top-quartile Gulf banks achieve 80%+ FCR
H3 | Training Time

New hire training exceeds 5 weeks due to 13-system landscape

Each system requires separate training. At 22% annual frontline turnover, DIB trains approximately 250 new employees per year. Reducing training from 5 weeks to 2 weeks saves significant onboarding cost.

Source: UAE banking industry; unified platform achieves 1-2 weeks
H4 | Digital Deflection

40-55% of branch visits and contact center calls could be self-served digitally

Balance inquiries, card management, profile changes, and basic transaction support are all candidates for digital deflection. Leading UAE digital banks achieve 60%+ self-service resolution.

Source: Emirates NBD and Mashreq benchmarks; GCC digital banking reports
H5 | Shariah Compliance Overhead

Separate Shariah compliance step adds 15-20% processing time to every financing product

Unlike conventional banks, DIB requires an additional compliance verification layer. Embedding this into guided workflows could eliminate 2-5 min per financing interaction without compromising compliance.

Source: Islamic banking operational benchmarks; DIB-specific hypothesis
H6 | Employee Satisfaction

Employee satisfaction with tools is below 50%, directly impacting retention

Tool frustration is the top driver of frontline disengagement in banking. Engaged employees deliver 21% higher productivity (Gallup). For DIB's premium service positioning, employee tool quality directly affects brand promise delivery.

Source: Gallup employee engagement research; Gulf banking HR benchmarks
