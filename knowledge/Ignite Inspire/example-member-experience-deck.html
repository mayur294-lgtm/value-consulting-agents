Fundamental Problems of the Current Digital Experience

Problem 1

Channel Fragmentation

OLB, mobile, and branch offer different capabilities and interfaces. A customer who starts a home finance application on OLB cannot continue it on mobile or in-branch without restarting the entire process.

Problem 2

Incomplete Digital Journeys

Finance applications (auto, home, personal), credit card applications, and complex servicing requests all require branch visits. Digital initiates but cannot close — creating high abandonment.

Problem 3

No Personalization

All 1.7 million customers see the same generic interface regardless of segment, product holdings, life stage, or relationship depth. A young professional sees the same experience as an established business owner.

Problem 4

Sharia Compliance Friction

Contract signing, Fatwa disclosures, Murabaha/Ijara structuring are not digitized — forcing branch visits even for tech-savvy customers. These are unique to Islamic banking and require specific digital solutions.

Problem 5

Reactive Service Model

No proactive notifications for finance payment reminders, rate changes, or life-event triggers. Customers only engage when something goes wrong, missing opportunities for value-add interactions.

Problem 6

Missing Financial Wellness

No budgeting tools, savings goals, Zakat calculator, or financial health scoring. Customers manage money outside the app using spreadsheets or competing fintech apps.

Cross-Cutting Challenges

  • Online Banking and mobile app offer different functionality, creating confusion for customers who use both
  • No cross-channel continuity: processes started in one channel cannot be resumed in another
  • Lack of personalization throughout the experience — all 1.7M customers see the same interface
  • Sharia-specific product features (profit rates, Murabaha structures) not well-explained in digital channels
  • Expatriate customers face additional friction due to document requirements not supported digitally
  • No unified customer profile across channels — branch and digital have separate views
  • Arabic language experience inconsistent across touchpoints; right-to-left layout issues in some flows
