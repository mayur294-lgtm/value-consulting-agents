## Temenos (Infinity/Quantum)

### Profile
- **Product Lines:** Temenos Infinity (digital), Temenos Quantum (digital + journey mgmt)
- **Market Position:** Core banking leader attempting digital expansion through M&A
- **Acquisition History:** Kony (2019), Avoka (2019) - creates integration confusion

### Capability Assessment

| Capability | Backbase | Temenos | Gap |
|------------|----------|---------|-----|
| Integrated Solution | 8.3 | 7.0 | +1.3 |
| Operating Model | 7.8 | 6.5 | +1.3 |
| Strategy & Roadmap | 7.8 | 6.5 | +1.3 |
| Solution Innovation | 7.5 | 6.0 | +1.5 |

### Strategic Positioning

| Aspect | Backbase | Temenos |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Approach** | Outside-In (Customer First) | Inside-Out (Core First) |
| **Focus** | Customer experience orchestration | Core banking with digital bolt-on |
| **Architecture** | True microservices, API-first | Legacy with modernization layers |
| **Implementation** | Bank-owned, SI-delivered | Vendor-dependent |

### Key Weaknesses to Exploit

1. **M&A Confusion**
   - Kony + Avoka acquisitions not fully integrated
   - Unclear roadmap between Infinity and Quantum
   - Customer confusion about which product to buy

2. **Implementation Track Record**
   - Poor Infinity implementation history
   - BDO Philippines case study (switched to Backbase)
   - Long implementation timelines

3. **Architecture Limitations**
   - Core-first thinking limits agility
   - Digital is bolt-on, not native
   - Limited true omnichannel capability

4. **Vendor Lock-in**
   - Heavy dependency on Temenos services
   - Limited SI ecosystem for digital
   - Expensive change requests

### Win Strategies

1. **Challenge with PoC** - Propose side-by-side proof of concept
2. **Reference Check** - Ask for Infinity-only references (not bundled with core)
3. **BDO Story** - Reference BDO Philippines journey
4. **Future-Proofing** - Emphasize architectural flexibility
5. **Total Cost** - Model 5-year TCO including changes

### Discovery Questions

- "How integrated is your digital experience with your core systems today?"
- "What's been your experience with Temenos digital implementations in your network?"
- "How quickly can you currently make changes to customer journeys?"
- "Is your digital strategy driven by customer needs or system constraints?"

## FIS (D1 / Digital One)

### Profile
- **Product:** Digital One (D1) - consolidated digital banking platform
- **Market Position:** Large incumbent with broad banking services portfolio
- **Strategy:** Leveraging core banking relationships for digital cross-sell

### Capability Assessment

| Capability | Backbase | FIS D1 | Gap |
|------------|----------|--------|-----|
| Integrated Solution | 8.3 | 6.0 | +2.3 |
| Operating Model | 7.8 | 6.0 | +1.8 |
| Strategy & Roadmap | 7.8 | 6.0 | +1.8 |
| Solution Innovation | 7.5 | 6.0 | +1.5 |
| **Overall** | **7.9** | **6.0** | **+1.9** |

### Key Weaknesses to Exploit

1. **Monolithic Architecture**
   - Single codebase limits flexibility
   - Changes require full regression testing
   - 12-week release cycles minimum

2. **Vendor Dependency**
   - All changes go through FIS
   - No real SDK for bank customization
   - Change requests: 6+ months for significant changes

3. **Implementation Timeline**
   - Average 18 months to production
   - Complex integration requirements
   - Heavy professional services dependency

4. **Limited Innovation**
   - Roadmap driven by largest clients
   - Slower to adopt new technologies
   - Feature parity challenges

### Competitive Comparison

| Dimension | Backbase | FIS D1 |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Time to Market** | ~12 months | ~18 months |
| **Change Velocity** | <40 days | 6+ months |
| **Release Frequency** | Continuous | 12-week cycles |
| **Customization** | Full SDK | Vendor-managed |
| **Integration** | API-first | Point-to-point |
| **Ownership** | Bank-owned IP | Vendor-controlled |

### Win Strategies

1. **Speed to Value** - Emphasize faster implementation and change velocity
2. **Total Control** - Bank ownership vs vendor dependency
3. **Architecture Deep-Dive** - Technical comparison of microservices vs monolithic
4. **Reference Visits** - Comparable bank references with metrics
5. **Innovation Pace** - Compare roadmap velocity and feature delivery

### Discovery Questions

- "How long does it take to make a change to your digital banking today?"
- "Who owns your digital IP - you or your vendor?"
- "How often can you release new features to customers?"
- "What's your experience with FIS responsiveness to change requests?"

## Q2

### Profile
- **Market Position:** Strong in North American mid-market
- **Deployment:** Primarily SaaS
- **Strength:** Fast initial implementation for standard deployments

### Capability Assessment

| Capability | Backbase | Q2 | Gap |
|------------|----------|-----|-----|

| Solution Innovation | 7.5 | 7.0 | +0.5 |
| **Overall** | **7.83** | **7.25** | **+0.58** |

### Strengths to Acknowledge

- Standard platform with faster initial go-live (7-8 months)
- Strong North American presence
- Good out-of-box functionality
- Established credit union relationships

### Key Weaknesses to Exploit

1. **Limited Customization**
   - No real SDK access
   - Configuration-only changes
   - "You get what you get" approach

2. **Static User Experience**
   - UX largely unchanged since 2020
   - Limited personalization capabilities
   - Mobile experience gaps

3. **Vendor Lock-in**
   - No SI ecosystem
   - All services through Q2
   - Change requests: >120 days

4. **Scalability Concerns**
   - Originally built for smaller FIs
   - Questions at Tier 1 scale
   - Performance at volume

## Key Differentiators Summary

### Architecture
- **Backbase:** True microservices, API-first, cloud-native
- **Competitors:** Various degrees of monolithic or hybrid architectures

### Operating Model
- **Backbase:** Bank-owned, SI ecosystem, full SDK
- **Competitors:** Vendor-dependent, limited customization, controlled roadmaps

### Speed & Agility
- **Backbase:** <40 days for changes, continuous releases
- **Competitors:** 3-6+ months for changes, quarterly releases at best

### Total Cost of Ownership
- **Backbase:** Higher initial investment, lower ongoing change costs
- **Competitors:** Variable initial costs, higher long-term change costs

### Strategic Flexibility
- **Backbase:** Multi-cloud, global, any core, any channel
- **Competitors:** Various limitations on deployment, geography, integration
