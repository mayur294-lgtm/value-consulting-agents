**Date:** 2026-01-13
**Prepared for:** Retail Bank - SEA Region
**Prepared by:** Value Consulting Team
**Planning Horizon:** 18 months (Months 0-18) + Ongoing

---

## Executive Summary

### Roadmap Overview

**Purpose:** Transform digital onboarding and servicing capabilities to improve customer acquisition (addressing low completion rates E1, E2) and reduce operational costs (addressing servicing inefficiency E4, E5) through automation, self-service, and data-driven optimization.

**Approach:** Phased, risk-managed approach starting with foundation and pilot (Months 0-6), scaling proven capabilities (Months 7-12), and optimizing for sustained value (Months 13-18+). Sequence balances quick wins, foundational infrastructure, and strategic transformation.

**Timeline:** 18 months from kickoff to full capability deployment, followed by continuous optimization

**Total Investment:** $8.0M implementation (Months 0-18) + $1.8M annual ongoing (Year 3+)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Customer acquisition improvement:** 30,000 additional customers annually through 25pp completion rate improvement (E1, E2 → $2.4M annual revenue)
- **Operational cost reduction:** $5.8M annual savings through 30pp digital containment improvement (E4, E5 → channel shift)
- **Foundation for innovation:** Journey analytics, automation platform, and API layer enable future AI/personalization capabilities

### Strategic Narrative

This roadmap addresses three critical business imperatives:

1. **Fix Customer Acquisition:** Onboarding completion rate is low (E1) with customers dropping off at document verification (E2) and cycle times exceeding 7 days (E3). This loses acquisition opportunities and revenue. We must fix this first to drive growth.

2. **Reduce Servicing Costs:** Servicing is expensive (E4) with simple requests routed to high-cost channels (E5) and no insight into channel behavior (E6). Digital self-service will shift 40,000 monthly transactions from $10 call center to $1 digital, saving $4-5M annually.

3. **Build Data-Driven Optimization Foundation:** Bank lacks journey visibility (E6) preventing systematic optimization. Analytics foundation enables measuring, learning, and continuous improvement.

The sequence reflects:
- **Value-first:** Highest ROI initiatives (self-service, onboarding) in Phases 1-2
- **Risk-managed:** Pilot before scaling; validate assumptions before full investment
- **Dependency-aware:** Foundation (workflow, APIs, analytics) must precede advanced capabilities
- **Capacity-realistic:** Phasing prevents overwhelming the organization; allows learning and iteration
- **Quick wins:** Deliver visible improvements in first 6 months to build momentum and confidence

**Guiding Principles:**
1. **Evidence-driven:** Every initiative traces to pain points (P1-P5) and evidence (E1-E8)
2. **Pilot then scale:** Validate in small scope before enterprise rollout
3. **Build on existing:** Enhance launched digital onboarding (E7), don't replace wholesale
4. **Measure and iterate:** Use journey analytics to guide optimization, not assumptions
5. **Adoption-focused:** Technology is necessary but insufficient; change management critical (E7, E8)

### Top 3 Risks Requiring Active Management

**Risk 1: Digital Adoption Challenges**
- **Description:** Customers and/or employees don't adopt digital capabilities despite availability, limiting benefit realization (similar to E7)
- **Impact:** Benefits reduced by 30-50% if adoption fails; ROI at risk
- **Mitigation:**
  - Invest 15-20% of budget in change management (Initiatives 2.4, 3.3)
  - Simple, intuitive UX (user testing, iteration)
  - Incentives for digital usage (fee waivers, rewards)
  - Executive mandate and employee KPIs
  - Pilot validates adoption before full rollout
- **Contingency:** If adoption low, pivot to assisted-digital (call center guides customer through digital), maintain some benefit
- **Owner:** Head of Retail, Change Management Lead

**Risk 2: Baseline Data Reveals Better Current State**
- **Description:** Data validation (Initiative 1.1) shows completion rate already 55% or containment already 40%, reducing benefit opportunity
- **Impact:** Revenue benefit reduced 50%+ if baseline already high
- **Mitigation:**
  - Accept this as good news - bank performing better than assumed
  - Adjust targets upward (if already 55%, target 75%)
  - Shift focus to cost reduction (more certain opportunity)
  - Expand scope to other journeys (lending, wealth) if onboarding already good
- **Contingency:** ROI remains positive even with reduced benefits (sensitivity analysis shows $0.8M NPV in worst case)
- **Owner:** Program Lead, Finance

**Risk 3: Regulatory Delays for eKYC**
- **Description:** Banking regulators require additional controls, testing, or reject digital identity approach for certain customer segments
- **Impact:** 3-6 month delay in onboarding automation; benefit realization delayed; potential cost overrun
- **Mitigation:**
  - Early engagement with regulators (Month 0-1)
  - Select compliant vendor with regional experience and regulator relationships
  - Hybrid approach: eKYC for low-risk customers, manual for high-risk (still achieves majority of benefit)
  - Manual fallback always available
- **Contingency:** Delay full rollout but proceed with self-service (independent of eKYC); shift timeline but maintain ROI
- **Owner:** Compliance Lead, Digital Lead

## Alternative Roadmap Approaches

### Alternative 1: Aggressive Timeline (12 months)

**Description:** Compress timeline by parallelizing more work and skipping pilot

**Approach:**
- Months 0-3: Foundation + market selection
- Months 4-9: Build and deploy to all markets simultaneously
- Months 10-12: Optimize

**Pros:**
- 6 months faster to full benefits ($3-4M earlier benefit realization)
- Lower implementation cost (shorter duration, less PM overhead)
- Faster competitive response

**Cons:**
- Higher execution risk (no pilot to validate and learn)
- Resource constraints (23+ FTE sustained vs. 15-23 phased)
- Higher probability of failure (no opportunity to course-correct)
- Potential quality issues and customer experience problems
- Change management inadequate (less time for adoption)

**Why Not Recommended:**
- Bank's track record shows execution challenges (E7 - digital onboarding "hasn't scaled well")
- Risk of large-scale failure too high given $8M investment
- Benefits of speed ($3M earlier) don't justify risk of failure (potential $8M loss)
- Better to deliver reliably on 18-month timeline than fail on 12-month

---

### Alternative 2: Pilot-Extended Approach (24 months)

**Description:** Run extended pilots in multiple markets before full rollout

**Approach:**
- Months 0-6: Foundation
- Months 7-18: Pilot in 3-5 markets with multiple iterations
- Months 19-24: Rollout to remaining markets

**Pros:**
- Lowest execution risk (extensive validation before full commitment)
- Highest quality (time to perfect before scale)
- Multiple A/B tests and optimizations
- Organizational readiness (more time for change management)

**Cons:**
- 6 months slower to full benefits ($4-5M delayed benefit realization)
- Higher opportunity cost (competitors may move faster)
- Potential organizational fatigue (2-year program)
- May over-optimize for pilot markets (not representative)

**Why Not Recommended:**
- Benefits delayed by $4-5M outweigh risk reduction (NPV impact: -$3M)
- Competitive market requires faster response (digital-first banks moving quickly)
- 18-month approach already includes 3-month pilot + 6-month phased rollout (adequate validation)
- Marginal risk reduction doesn't justify 6-month delay

---

### Alternative 3: Self-Service First, Onboarding Later (Inverted Sequence)

**Description:** Prioritize self-service and cost reduction; defer onboarding to Phase 2

**Approach:**
- Phase 1: Self-service expansion and channel shift (Months 0-9)
- Phase 2: Onboarding automation (Months 10-18)

**Pros:**
- Faster ROI (self-service payback 14 months vs. onboarding 16 months)
- Larger benefit stream first ($5.8M vs. $2.4M)
- Easier change management (internal operations vs. customer-facing)
- De-risks customer-facing onboarding changes

**Cons:**
- Misaligns with stakeholder priorities (Head of Retail prioritizes customer acquisition E1)
- Harder to justify digital investment without revenue growth story
- Onboarding improvements create better foundation for self-service adoption (good onboarding → engaged customers → more self-service usage)
- Competitive disadvantage continues (poor onboarding loses customers to competitors)

**Why Not Recommended:**
- Strategic priorities favor customer acquisition over cost reduction
- Onboarding and self-service share common foundation (workflow, analytics) - limited benefit to separating
- Phased approach already sequences to deliver both within 12 months
- Stakeholder alignment and momentum require addressing onboarding pain (E1, E2, E3)
