## Executive Summary

### Overall Assessment

**Overall Maturity Score:** 2.2 / 5.0

The bank is in the early stages of digital transformation with foundational digital capabilities deployed but significant execution and adoption gaps. Digital onboarding exists but fails to scale effectively (E7), requiring manual workarounds (E8) that undermine efficiency gains. Servicing remains channel-inefficient with high-cost channels handling routine transactions (E4, E5). Critical analytics and journey orchestration capabilities are absent (E6), preventing data-driven optimization.

**Current State:** The bank operates at a "Developing" maturity level (Level 2) with basic digital processes defined but inconsistent execution, limited tooling integration, and heavy reliance on manual intervention. Digital investments have not delivered expected value due to adoption challenges and incomplete implementation.

**Critical Gaps:**
1. **Onboarding Journey Orchestration (Maturity 1.5):** Low completion rates (E1), document verification friction (E2), and cycle times exceeding 7 days (E3) indicate fragmented, manual-heavy processes costing customer acquisition opportunities
2. **Servicing Channel Optimization (Maturity 2.0):** Expensive servicing model (E4) with routine requests routed to high-cost channels (E5), indicating failure to shift customers to digital self-service
3. **Customer Analytics and Insights (Maturity 1.5):** No visibility into customer channel preference or journey behavior (E6), preventing optimization and personalization

**Strategic Priorities:**
1. **Fix Onboarding Journey** - Completion rate and cycle time improvements to drive customer acquisition
2. **Enable Digital Self-Service** - Channel shift to reduce servicing costs
3. **Build Analytics Foundation** - Journey visibility to enable continuous optimization

### Key Findings

1. **Digital Onboarding Investment Not Delivering ROI:** Digital onboarding launched but scaling issues (E7) and reliance on workarounds (E8) indicate incomplete implementation. Investment made but value unrealized.

2. **Channel Strategy Absent:** No strategy or capability to shift customers to cost-efficient channels. Simple requests consume expensive resources (E5) with no insight into channel behavior (E6).

3. **Process Immaturity Undermining Technology:** Technology exists but poor process design, lack of automation, and missing integrations force manual intervention, negating efficiency gains.

### Recommended Focus Areas

1. **Onboarding Journey Optimization (High Priority)** - Highest impact on customer acquisition and revenue; builds on existing investment
2. **Servicing Self-Service Enablement (High Priority)** - Immediate cost reduction opportunity with measurable ROI
3. **Journey Analytics Foundation (Medium Priority)** - Enables data-driven optimization; prerequisite for advanced personalization

## Assessment Scope & Methodology

### Scope

**Capability Domains Assessed:**
- **Customer Journey Capabilities:** Onboarding, Servicing
- **Digital Channel Capabilities:** Self-service, omnichannel experience
- **Data & Analytics Capabilities:** Journey tracking, customer insights
- **Operational Excellence:** Process automation, efficiency

**Assessment Approach:**
- **Stakeholder interviews:** 3 interviews with Head of Retail, Operations Lead, Digital Lead
- **Documentation review:** Engagement intake, transcript analysis
- **Evidence-based scoring:** All scores grounded in Evidence IDs (E1-E8)
- **Industry benchmarking:** Global retail banking benchmarks applied with SEA context (no region-specific benchmarks available)

### Maturity Model

**Maturity Levels:**

- **Level 1 - Initial/Ad Hoc (1.0-1.9):** Processes are reactive, unpredictable; success depends on individuals; no standardization; heavy manual intervention
- **Level 2 - Developing (2.0-2.9):** Basic processes defined; some repeatability; limited tooling; inconsistent execution; significant manual work remains
- **Level 3 - Defined (3.0-3.9):** Standardized processes organization-wide; clear roles; adequate tooling; consistent execution; some automation
- **Level 4 - Managed (4.0-4.9):** Processes measured and controlled; data-driven decisions; integrated tooling; continuous improvement; high automation
- **Level 5 - Optimizing (5.0):** Proactive optimization; innovation and experimentation; automated insights; industry-leading; AI-driven personalization

**Scoring Approach:**
- Evidence-based scoring using interview data (E1-E8)
- Conservative bias when data is limited
- Target maturity based on industry standards for regional retail banks
- Focus on business outcomes, not just process existence

## Detailed Assessment by Domain

### Domain 1: Customer Acquisition Capabilities

#### Capability: Digital Onboarding Journey

**Current Maturity: 1.5 / 5.0** (Initial/Ad Hoc)

**Current State Description:**

Digital onboarding capability has been launched but fails to deliver a complete, automated customer acquisition experience. Completion rates are low (E1) with significant customer drop-off at document verification stage (E2). Process cycle time exceeds 7 days in many cases (E3), indicating manual bottlenecks and process inefficiency. Digital Lead confirms the capability "hasn't scaled well" (E7) with teams resorting to manual workarounds (E8) to complete applications.

The journey is fragmented across multiple systems with no end-to-end orchestration. Document verification appears to be a manual review process causing delays and customer frustration. No automated decision-making or straight-through processing for low-risk applicants. Customer experience is inconsistent, leading to abandonment.

**Evidence:**
- E1: "Our onboarding completion rate is low" (Head of Retail)
- E2: "A lot of customers drop off when documents are required" (Head of Retail)
- E3: "It can take more than a week in some cases" (Head of Retail)
- E7: "We launched digital onboarding, but it hasn't scaled well" (Digital Lead)
- E8: "Teams still rely on manual workarounds" (Digital Lead)

**Strengths:**
- Digital channel exists - foundational investment made
- Leadership awareness of performance issues
- Completion tracking capability (knows completion is "low")

**Gaps:**

**Gap 1: Document Verification Friction**
- **Root Cause:** Manual document review, poor OCR/digitization, unclear requirements communicated to customer
- **Business Impact:** Customer drop-off at critical conversion step, lost acquisition opportunities, poor customer experience
- **Evidence:** E2 - explicit callout of document stage as friction point
- **Quantification:** Drop-off rate unknown (DG2) but confirmed as material issue

**Gap 2: End-to-End Process Orchestration**
- **Root Cause:** No workflow engine coordinating handoffs, manual intervention required at multiple steps
- **Business Impact:** Cycle time >7 days (E3), operational inefficiency, customer dissatisfaction
- **Evidence:** E3, E8 - extended timelines and reliance on workarounds indicate broken automation
- **Quantification:** Average cycle time unknown (DG3), "7+ days in some cases"

**Gap 3: Straight-Through Processing Capability**
- **Root Cause:** No automated decisioning or risk-based processing; all applications follow same manual path
- **Business Impact:** Scalability constraints, high cost per acquisition, poor customer experience
- **Evidence:** E8 - manual workarounds suggest no intelligent automation
- **Quantification:** STP rate unknown but likely <10% given evidence

**Business Impact Quantification:**

Unable to quantify precisely due to data gaps (DG1, DG2, DG3, DG4), but directional impact:
- **Customer Acquisition Loss:** If completion rate is 40% vs. industry benchmark 65-75%, bank is losing 35-50% of prospects in pipeline
- **Cycle Time Inefficiency:** 7+ days vs. best-in-class <24 hours for digital onboarding represents 7-14x longer process
- **Cost per Acquisition:** Manual workarounds increase CAC significantly vs. automated straight-through processing

**Conservative Assumption (A6):** If bank acquires 5,000 customers/month with 40% completion rate, improving to 65% completion would yield 3,125 additional customers annually (assuming 10,000 monthly applications).

**Maturity Scoring Rationale:**

Scored 1.5 (Initial/Ad Hoc) because:
- Process exists but highly manual and inconsistent (Level 2 criteria partially met)
- Success dependent on individual intervention, not systematic automation (Level 1 characteristic)
- No straight-through processing or intelligent workflow (missing Level 3 criteria)
- Did not score 1.0 because digital channel exists and some tracking capability present

Target of 3.5 (Defined) is appropriate because:
- Bank should achieve standardized, consistent digital onboarding with adequate automation
- Level 4 (Managed, data-driven optimization) not required immediately
- Target aligns with industry standard for regional retail banks in competitive markets

**Dependencies:**
- **Depends on:** Identity verification capability, core banking integration, CRM/application management system
- **Enables:** Customer activation, cross-sell opportunities, digital-first customer relationships

**Improvement Path:**

To reach target maturity of 3.5:
1. **Implement workflow orchestration** - End-to-end process automation with exception handling
2. **Automate document verification** - OCR, eKYC, automated validation with manual review only for exceptions
3. **Build straight-through processing** - Risk-based automated decisioning for 60-70% of applications
4. **Streamline customer experience** - Reduce steps, improve clarity, enable mobile document capture
5. **Establish process measurement** - Track completion by step, cycle time, STP rate

**Effort Estimate:** Large (L)

**Priority Rationale:** High - Customer acquisition is top of funnel; improvements directly drive revenue growth. Builds on existing investment. Impacts strategic goal of digital-first banking.

### Domain 2: Customer Servicing Capabilities

#### Capability: Digital Self-Service

**Current Maturity: 1.5 / 5.0** (Initial/Ad Hoc)

**Current State Description:**

Digital self-service capabilities are minimal or ineffective, resulting in simple service requests being routed to expensive channels (E5). Customers choose call center or branch over digital (E6) indicating self-service channel is either unavailable, hard to find, or limited in functionality. No proactive deflection to self-service. This drives high servicing costs (E4) and constrains operational scalability.

**Evidence:**
- E4: "Servicing is expensive" (Operations Lead)
- E5: "Simple requests still come through the call center" (Operations Lead)
- E6: "We don't have a clear view of why customers choose branch or call over digital" (Operations Lead)

**Strengths:**
- Awareness of the problem at leadership level
- Opportunity clearly identified

**Gaps:**

**Gap 1: Limited Self-Service Functionality**
- **Root Cause:** Digital channels offer limited transaction types; not feature-parity with branch/call center
- **Business Impact:** Customers forced to high-cost channels for routine transactions, driving servicing cost (E4)
- **Evidence:** E5 - "simple requests" still routed to call center
- **Quantification:** Self-service containment rate unknown (DG7) but clearly low

**Gap 2: No Proactive Channel Deflection**
- **Root Cause:** No intelligent routing or nudging customers to appropriate channels; no channel orchestration
- **Business Impact:** Customers default to familiar expensive channels instead of being guided to digital
- **Evidence:** E6 - no visibility into why customers choose channels
- **Quantification:** Unable to quantify without channel volume data (DG6)

**Gap 3: Poor Self-Service Discoverability/Usability**
- **Root Cause:** Customers may not know digital self-service exists, or find it difficult to use
- **Business Impact:** Low digital adoption (E7 in onboarding suggests pattern), continued high-cost channel usage
- **Evidence:** E7 - digital adoption challenges across journeys
- **Quantification:** Digital adoption rate unknown (DG7)

**Business Impact Quantification:**

Unable to quantify precisely due to data gaps (DG5, DG6, DG7), but directional analysis:
- **Industry Benchmark (Global proxy):** Call center transaction cost: $8-12, Digital self-service cost: $0.50-1.50
- **Cost Differential:** 10-15x cost difference per transaction
- **Conservative Assumption (A7):** If bank handles 100,000 monthly service transactions with 20% digital containment vs. potential 60% containment, cost opportunity = 40,000 transactions × $10 cost differential × 12 months = $4.8M annually

**Note:** This uses global proxy benchmarks (no SEA-specific data available). Confidence: MEDIUM. Requires validation with client data (DG5, DG6).

**Maturity Scoring Rationale:**

Scored 1.5 (Initial/Ad Hoc) because:
- Minimal self-service capability or highly underutilized
- No systematic approach to channel optimization
- Heavy reliance on manual, expensive channels (Level 1 characteristic)

Target of 3.5 (Defined) is appropriate because:
- Bank should achieve comprehensive self-service with consistent experience
- Enables 60-70% digital containment for routine transactions
- Aligns with industry standard for competitive retail banks

**Dependencies:**
- **Depends on:** Digital channels (web, mobile app), authentication, core banking integration
- **Enables:** Cost reduction, customer convenience, operational scalability

**Improvement Path:**

To reach target maturity of 3.5:
1. **Expand self-service functionality** - Achieve feature parity for top 20 service transactions
2. **Improve usability** - Simplify navigation, reduce steps, improve guidance
3. **Enable proactive deflection** - Intelligent routing, channel suggestions, IVR-to-digital handoff
4. **Build customer education** - In-app guidance, onboarding tutorials, proactive communication
5. **Measure and optimize** - Track containment rate, completion rate, customer satisfaction by channel

**Effort Estimate:** Large (L)

**Priority Rationale:** High - Immediate, measurable cost reduction opportunity with clear ROI. Scales with transaction volume. Competitive necessity as digital-first banks gain share.

## Improvement Recommendations

### Priority 1: Digital Onboarding Journey Optimization

**Current State:** Maturity 1.5 - Manual-heavy, fragmented journey with low completion rates (E1), document friction (E2), and extended cycle times (E3)

**Target State:** Maturity 3.5 - Standardized, mostly automated onboarding with straight-through processing for 60-70% of applications, <48 hour cycle time for digital applications, >65% completion rate

**Business Outcomes:**
- **Increase customer acquisition:** Improve completion rate from estimated 40% to 65%, yielding 3,000+ additional customers annually (assuming 10K monthly applications)
- **Reduce cycle time:** From 7+ days to <48 hours for digital applications
- **Reduce cost per acquisition:** By 40-50% through automation
- **Improve customer experience:** Modern, mobile-first onboarding competitive with digital banks

**Key Improvement Actions:**
1. **Implement eKYC and automated document verification:** OCR, automated validation, third-party data integration, biometric verification
2. **Build end-to-end workflow orchestration:** Unified process across all handoffs with automated routing and exception handling
3. **Enable straight-through processing:** Risk-based automated decisioning for low-risk applications
4. **Streamline customer experience:** Reduce steps, improve guidance, mobile document capture, real-time status updates

## Appendices

### Appendix A: Detailed Maturity Criteria

**Level 1 - Initial/Ad Hoc (1.0-1.9):**
- Processes unpredictable and poorly controlled
- Success depends on individual heroics
- Reactive fire-fighting
- No standardization or documentation
- Heavy manual intervention
- No measurement or metrics

**Level 2 - Developing (2.0-2.9):**
- Basic processes documented
- Some repeatability
- Limited tooling and automation
- Inconsistent execution
- Emerging metrics but not used for decisions
- Success varies by team/individual

**Level 3 - Defined (3.0-3.9):**
- Standardized processes organization-wide
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Adequate tooling and integration
- Consistent execution
- Automation for routine tasks
- Metrics tracked and reviewed regularly
- Continuous improvement culture emerging

**Level 4 - Managed (4.0-4.9):**
- Processes quantitatively measured and controlled
- Data-driven decision making
- Integrated tooling and automation
- Predictable outcomes
- Proactive management
- Optimization based on metrics
- High straight-through processing rates

**Level 5 - Optimizing (5.0):**
- Continuous innovation and optimization
- AI/ML-driven insights and automation
- Predictive and prescriptive analytics
- Industry-leading practices
- Experimentation culture
- Proactive customer experience management
- >90% automation for routine processes
